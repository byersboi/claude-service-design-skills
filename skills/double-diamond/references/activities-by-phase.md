# Activities by Phase — Double Diamond

A comprehensive reference of activities, methods, and outputs for each phase of the Double Diamond.

---

## Phase 1: Discover

### Research Methods

| Method | Best for | Duration | Participants |
|--------|----------|----------|--------------|
| Semi-structured interviews | Deep understanding of motivations, behaviours, context | 45–90 min each | 6–15 users |
| Contextual inquiry | Observing real behaviour in real environments | 1–3 hours on-site | 5–10 users |
| Diary studies | Longitudinal behaviour, over-time patterns | 1–4 weeks | 8–15 participants |
| Cultural probes | Sensitive topics, hard-to-reach audiences | 2–3 weeks | 5–10 participants |
| Shadowing / fly on the wall | Front-line staff behaviour, service delivery | 2–8 hours | Staff at multiple sites |
| Expert interviews | Domain knowledge, system constraints | 30–60 min | 3–8 experts |
| Focus groups | Group dynamics, community perspectives | 90 min | 6–8 participants |
| Survey | Quantitative validation, scale | Ongoing | 50–500+ |
| Desk research | Secondary data, existing evidence | Variable | — |
| Mystery shopping | First-hand experience of your own service | 1–2 hours | 2–5 researchers |

### Discovery Workshop Activities

- **Assumption mapping:** Write all team assumptions on sticky notes, cluster them, identify which are riskiest and most untested
- **Research question prioritisation:** Dot vote on the most important questions to answer in research
- **Stakeholder mapping:** Identify who to talk to and in what order
- **Safari:** Send team members to experience the service (or analogous services) firsthand

### Discovery Outputs

- Interview transcripts or notes
- Observation notes and photos
- Research debrief summaries
- Raw stimuli (photos, artefacts, videos)
- Initial assumptions log (for comparison later)

## Phase 2: Define

### Synthesis Methods

| Method | Purpose | When to use |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| Affinity mapping | Cluster raw data into themes | After research, before insight writing |
| Thematic analysis | Identify patterns across multiple data sources | Complex research sets |
| Empathy mapping | Capture says/thinks/does/feels per user | When building personas |
| Jobs-to-be-done | Identify core functional and emotional goals | When motivations matter more than demographics |
| Experience mapping | Map the existing end-to-end experience | When the current state is complex |
| Insight statements | Capture non-obvious truths from research | Before writing HMW questions |

### Define Workshop Activities

- **Download session:** Debrief after research. Each researcher shares key observations before patterns are discussed.
- **Affinity mapping:** All findings on sticky notes, clustered into themes, then labelled with insights.
- **Insight to HMW:** Transform insights into How Might We questions by reframing as opportunities.
- **Point of View (POV) madlib:** "[User] needs [need] because [insight]" — grounds the design challenge in evidence.
- **Design criteria setting:** What must be true of any solution? What constraints apply?

### Insight Statement Formula

> "[User type] + [verb] + [observation/need] + because [non-obvious insight]"

Example:
> "NHS outpatients need to feel in control of their appointment time because uncertainty about waiting creates more anxiety than the wait itself."

### HMW Question Formula

> "How might we [verb phrase that reframes the problem as an opportunity]?"

Example:
> "How might we give outpatients a sense of progress during long waits?"

### Define Outputs

- Affinity map / insight clusters
- Insight statements (3–8 key insights)
- How Might We questions
- Point of View statements
- User personas or archetypes
- Current-state journey map
- Design brief / design challenge
- Design principles (optional but valuable)

## Phase 3: Develop

### Ideation Methods

| Method | Energy | Group size | Duration |
|--------|--------|------------|----------|
| Brainstorming | High | 3–8 | 20–45 min |
| Crazy 8s | High | Individual | 8 min |
| SCAMPER | Medium | Individual/pairs | 20–30 min |
| Worst possible idea | High | Any | 15–20 min |
| Analogies and metaphors | Medium | 4–8 | 30 min |
| Brainwriting (6-3-5) | Low-medium | 6 | 30 min |
| Round-robin | Low | Any | 20 min |
| SWOT on concepts | Medium | Pairs | 20 min per concept |

### Concept Development Activities

- **Concept sketching:** Turn ideas into rough visualisations — not wireframes, but enough to communicate the concept
- **Concept card:** Name, tagline, description, sketch, key questions — on one A4 sheet
- **Storyboarding:** Narrate the concept as a sequence of scenes showing how a user experiences it
- **Value proposition canvas:** Map what the concept does for users vs what it requires of them
- **10x10 matrix:** Take 10 ideas and push each 10 steps further — breaks incremental thinking

### Prototyping in Develop

| Prototype type | Fidelity | Best for |
|----------------|----------|---------|
| Paper sketch | Very low | Communicating the concept quickly |
| Desktop walkthrough | Low | Simulating service flow with props on a table |
| Role play / experience prototype | Low–medium | Testing emotional and behavioural responses |
| Storyboard | Low | Communicating context and narrative |
| Wizard of Oz | Medium | Testing digital interfaces before building them |
| Pilot | High | Testing the full service in real conditions |

### Develop Outputs

- Idea long-list (diverge)
- Shortlisted concepts (converge)
- Concept cards or concept decks
- Prototypes (multiple fidelity levels)
- Test insights (from prototype testing)
- Refined concept with evidence of testing
- Future-state journey map
- Future-state service blueprint (draft)

## Phase 4: Deliver

### Delivery Activities

| Activity | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Detailed service blueprinting | Document the full service model for implementation |
| Pilot design | Plan a bounded test of the full service |
| Pilot evaluation | Measure the pilot against defined success criteria |
| Implementation roadmapping | Sequence the changes required to implement the service |
| Capability assessment | Identify what skills, systems, or processes need to change |
| Handover documentation | Ensure the service can be operated without the design team |
| Measurement framework | Define how success will be tracked over time |

### Delivery Outputs

- Pilot plan and evaluation report
- Refined service blueprint (implementation-ready)
- Implementation roadmap
- Service manual or operations guide
- Measurement framework and baseline metrics
- Stakeholder presentation / business case
- Handover pack

## Phase Gates (Transition Criteria)

### Discover → Define
- [ ] Research conducted with real users (not just internal stakeholders)
- [ ] Key findings documented and shared with the team
- [ ] Team has reached saturation (hearing same themes repeatedly)
- [ ] Research questions have been answered (or explicitly deferred)

### Define → Develop
- [ ] Research has been synthesised into insight statements
- [ ] A clear problem statement / design challenge is agreed
- [ ] The team is aligned on who they are designing for
- [ ] How Might We questions have been generated from insights

### Develop → Deliver
- [ ] Multiple distinct concepts have been generated and evaluated
- [ ] At least one concept has been tested with real users
- [ ] A chosen concept has been validated and refined through iteration
- [ ] The team is confident the concept solves the defined problem

### Deliver → Live
- [ ] A pilot has been run and evaluated
- [ ] Key risks have been identified and mitigated
- [ ] An implementation owner has been identified
- [ ] A measurement plan is in place
