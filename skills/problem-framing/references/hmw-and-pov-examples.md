# HMW and POV Examples by Service Domain

Extended examples of Point of View statements and How Might We questions across service types.

---

## POV Statement Formula

> "[User] needs [need] because [non-obvious insight]."

The "because" clause must be non-obvious. If it could have been written without research, it is not an insight.

---

## Healthcare

### POV Statements

> "Patients waiting for outpatient appointments need a sense of progress and control because uncertainty about duration creates more anxiety than the wait itself — and without information, they imagine the worst."

> "Carers managing multiple services for a family member need a single point of contact because navigating fragmented services is itself a full-time job that carers are doing without pay, training, or recognition."

> "First-time mental health service users need to understand what 'talking therapy' will actually involve because their prior associations with therapy come from TV and film — and the reality is often nothing like their expectations."

> "Elderly patients leaving hospital need a supported transition home because the gap between being discharged and feeling 'back to normal' is longer and harder than clinical staff realise — and families are not always equipped to fill it."

### How Might We Questions

**From insight: "Uncertainty creates more anxiety than the wait itself"**
- How might we give patients a real-time sense of where they are in the queue?
- How might we help patients understand what to expect before their appointment?
- How might we redesign the waiting space so it feels less like limbo?

**From insight: "Carers are doing a full-time job without support"**
- How might we reduce the coordination burden on carers managing complex care needs?
- How might we make the full picture of a person's care visible to all the services involved?
- How might we acknowledge and support the carer, not just the person being cared for?

## Government Services

### POV Statements

> "First-time benefit claimants need reassurance that they are doing the right thing because the fear of making an error — and not knowing the consequences — is more disabling than the process itself."

> "Recent immigrants applying for public services need clarity about what they are entitled to because eligibility rules are complex, change frequently, and the consequences of getting it wrong feel catastrophic."

> "People in housing crisis need a single, clear next step because when everything is urgent and nothing is clear, the result is paralysis — not action."

> "Front-line staff at job centres need to give people honest information because they currently manage the tension between policy constraints and what claimants actually need by avoiding direct answers — which creates more confusion, not less."

### How Might We Questions

**From insight: "Fear of making an error is more disabling than the process"**
- How might we help first-time claimants feel confident they've done everything correctly before they submit?
- How might we make it clear what will happen after they submit — and when?
- How might we reduce the perceived cost of making a mistake?

**From insight: "When everything is urgent, the result is paralysis"**
- How might we help people in crisis identify the single most important action to take right now?
- How might we design an entry point that feels manageable even when someone is overwhelmed?
- How might we help people in crisis feel that someone is on their side?

## Financial Services

### POV Statements

> "People who have never claimed insurance need a clear narrative of the process because they are used to buying products, not navigating claims — and the gap between expectation and reality damages trust at the most vulnerable moment."

> "Small business owners managing their finances need tools that fit into existing habits because they are not accountants — they are people running a business who deal with finances because they have to, not because they want to."

> "People seeking financial advice for the first time need to feel that the advisor is on their side because they approach the conversation assuming that advice will lead to a product sale — and this assumption shapes what they disclose."

### How Might We Questions

**From insight: "They approach advice assuming a product sale"**
- How might we design the first conversation so that people feel the advisor is working for them, not for a commission?
- How might we help people come prepared with the right questions?
- How might we make financial advice feel less like a transaction and more like a conversation?

---

## Education

### POV Statements

> "Students struggling with coursework need to ask for help before they reach crisis point, but they don't because asking feels like admitting they shouldn't be here — and the fear of that judgement is greater than the fear of failing."

> "Parents supporting children with learning differences need simple, jargon-free guidance from schools because the language of special educational needs is accessible only to those already embedded in the system — and it functions as a barrier rather than a bridge."

### How Might We Questions

**From insight: "Asking for help feels like admitting they shouldn't be here"**
- How might we normalise help-seeking as a sign of engagement, not weakness?
- How might we design low-stakes access to support that doesn't require a formal declaration of struggle?
- How might we make it easier for students to ask a small question before it becomes a big problem?

## Reframing Examples

| Initial problem framing | Insight | Reframed HMW |
|------------------------|---------|-------------|
| "How do we get more people to complete the online form?" | People abandon not because they can't use the form but because they hit a question they can't answer and don't know where to find the information. | How might we help people gather everything they need before they start? |
| "How do we reduce repeat contacts?" | Repeat contacts happen not because people don't understand the first answer but because the answer changes based on new developments in their situation. | How might we help people know when they need to contact us again, rather than guessing? |
| "How do we improve staff satisfaction scores?" | Staff satisfaction falls not because of the work but because staff feel they can't help people as well as they want to — the system constrains good service delivery. | How might we give staff the tools and flexibility they need to give people the help they actually need? |
| "How do we improve the digital channel adoption rate?" | Many users try the digital channel, encounter an obstacle, and switch to calling — not because they prefer the phone but because the digital channel feels less reliable. | How might we make the digital channel feel as safe and reliable as calling? |
