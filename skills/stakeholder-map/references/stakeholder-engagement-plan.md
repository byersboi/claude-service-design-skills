## Communication Channels by Quadrant

### Manage Closely
- Regular 1:1 meetings (fortnightly or weekly for critical periods)
- Personal briefings before significant decisions
- Invited to co-design workshops as participants or observers
- Direct access to design team — named point of contact

### Keep Satisfied
- Monthly written updates (1-page briefing)
- Quarterly steering group or review meeting
- Advance notice of significant findings or decisions
- Occasional 1:1 meetings when relevant

### Keep Informed
- Regular newsletter or project update (monthly)
- Workshop invitations (as users, not just observers)
- Clear channel for feedback and questions
- Visible opportunities to contribute

### Monitor
- Included on project newsletter
- Notified of significant milestones
- Check in at phase transitions

## Engagement Principles

**Be honest about what you don't know yet.** Stakeholders disengage when they feel they are being managed rather than engaged. Acknowledging uncertainty builds trust.

**Listen before you present.** Every stakeholder meeting should involve more listening than talking. Ask about their priorities, pressures, and concerns before explaining the project.

**Make it easy to contribute.** People engage when participation is low-friction and their input has visible impact. Show stakeholders how their feedback has shaped the work.

**Manage expectations around timelines.** Be clear about when decisions are needed and what happens if they are delayed.

**Document commitments.** After significant meetings, send a brief summary of what was discussed, what was agreed, and what the next steps are.
