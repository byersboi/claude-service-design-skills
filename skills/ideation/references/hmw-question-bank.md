# How Might We Question Bank

Examples of strong HMW questions across service types and challenge areas.

---

## What Makes a Strong HMW Question

| Quality | Good example | Weak example |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Specific enough to direct ideation | "How might we help first-time applicants feel confident they haven't made a mistake?" | "How might we improve the application?" |
| Open enough to allow varied solutions | "How might we give patients a sense of control during waiting?" | "How might we add a queue management display?" |
| Grounded in an insight | "How might we help carers ask for support without feeling like they've failed?" | "How might we market our support services better?" |
| Action-oriented | "How might we reduce the coordination burden on families managing complex care?" | "How might we make things easier?" |

---

## Healthcare

**Waiting and uncertainty:**
- How might we give patients a sense of control and progress during long waits?
- How might we help patients understand what "waiting for results" really means?
- How might we reduce the anxiety patients feel when they don't know what comes next?

**Information and communication:**
- How might we make appointment letters feel like they were written for the patient who receives them?
- How might we help patients hold onto and act on the information they receive in consultations?
- How might we ensure the things patients worry about are addressed before they leave?

**Access and navigation:**
- How might we help patients find the right service the first time?
- How might we reduce the burden on carers who manage multiple services for the same person?
- How might we make self-referral feel achievable for people who have never done it before?

---

## Government Services

**Digital self-service:**
- How might we help first-time users feel confident that they've done everything right?
- How might we make the application process feel achievable for people with lower digital literacy?
- How might we reduce the need for people to call when something unexpected happens in the form?

**Complex, multi-stage processes:**
- How might we help people understand where they are in the process and what happens next?
- How might we reduce the number of times people have to re-explain their situation to different parts of the service?
- How might we help people prepare everything they need before they start, not halfway through?

**Trust and legitimacy:**
- How might we help people feel confident that this service is legitimate and safe?
- How might we make the service feel like it is on the applicant's side?
- How might we acknowledge people's anxiety about the outcome without making it worse?

---

## Financial Services

**Onboarding:**
- How might we help new customers understand what they've signed up for without reading 40 pages?
- How might we make the first 30 days feel supported rather than overwhelming?

**Customer effort:**
- How might we help customers resolve their issue without having to call?
- How might we make it easy to get help from a human when self-service has failed?
- How might we prevent the frustration of being transferred multiple times?

**Trust during difficult moments:**
- How might we make a complaint feel like a conversation rather than an adversarial process?
- How might we help customers feel heard even when we can't give them the answer they want?

---

## Retail and E-commerce

**Pre-purchase:**
- How might we help customers feel confident they are choosing the right product?
- How might we make product information available in the format that works for this customer?

**Delivery and post-purchase:**
- How might we make delivery uncertainty feel manageable rather than frustrating?
- How might we make returns feel like a non-event rather than a deterrent to purchase?

---

## Education and Learning

**Onboarding students:**
- How might we help new students feel like they belong before they arrive?
- How might we reduce the administrative burden that students experience in the first week?

**Learning support:**
- How might we make it easy for students to ask for help without feeling like they should already know?
- How might we help students understand what support is available before they reach crisis point?

---

## Workplace and HR Services

**Employee experience:**
- How might we make it easy for managers to give meaningful feedback in the flow of work?
- How might we help employees understand their benefits without having to become HR experts?

**Onboarding:**
- How might we help new employees feel productive from day one?
- How might we ensure new starters know who to ask for help and feel comfortable asking?

## Adapting HMW Questions by Ambition Level

Take any insight and write HMWs at three levels of ambition:

**Example insight:** "Patients feel anxious before appointments because they don't know what to expect."

| Level | HMW question |
|-------|-------------|
| Incremental | How might we give patients a clearer description of what their appointment will involve? |
| Substantive | How might we help patients feel prepared and in control before they arrive? |
| Transformational | How might we redesign the pre-appointment experience so that anxiety is replaced with anticipation? |

Running ideation against all three levels produces a more varied concept set.
